# Chicken Noodle Soup

*Prep: 30 min | Cook: 1 hr 30 min | Total: 2 hrs | Serves: 6-8*

**Nutrition (per serving):** 385 cal | 14g fat | 32g carbs | 32g protein

**Full Stock:**
- 1000g (2.2 lb) chicken backs
- 750g (1.65 lb) chicken feet
- 2700g (11 cups) chicken stock

**Quick Stock:**
- 2700g chicken stock
- 30g Better Than Bouillon chicken
- 3 packets gelatin

**Soup:**
- 200g leeks or onion, sliced
- 400g carrots, sliced
- 300g celery, sliced
- 30g chicken fat or oil
- 550g (1.2 lb) chicken thighs
- 10g salt, plus more to taste
- Black pepper

**Noodles:**
- 225-300g egg noodles
- Salt

**Instructions:**
1. Roast chicken backs and feet at 425Â°F for about 25 minutes until lightly browned
2. Transfer to pressure cooker, deglaze roasting tray with water, add stock
3. Pressure-cook 45-60 minutes, strain, skim excess fat (save about 30g) or make quick stock
4. Salt chicken thighs and rest 30 minutes
5. Boil noodles in salted water per package directions; drain and toss in oil
6. Heat large pot on medium-high, add chicken fat, leeks, carrots, celery, and pinch of salt; sweat 5 minutes
7. Add stock, bring to gentle simmer
8. Add thighs, poach 15-17 minutes until tender
9. Remove chicken, shred and lightly chop, return to soup
10. Taste and season broth well
11. Serve soup over noodles with lots of black pepper

**Notes:**
- Chicken feet add amazing gelatin and body to the stock
- Make stock ahead and refrigerate - fat will solidify on top for easy removal
- Cooked noodles can be stored separately and added when serving to prevent them from getting mushy
