# Dutch Apple Pie with Crumb Topping

*Serves 8 | Prep: 35 min | Cook: 55 min | Cool: 2 hours*

**Apple Filling:**
- 5-6 apples (2½-2¾ pounds), peeled, cored, and sliced thinly
- 1 lemon, juiced
- ¼ cup all-purpose flour
- ½ cup granulated sugar
- 2 teaspoons cinnamon
- ¼ teaspoon ground cloves
- ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
- 1 9-inch pie crust (homemade or store-bought)

**Crumb Topping:**
- ½ cup butter, softened
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- ⅔ cup packed brown sugar
- 1 teaspoon cinnamon
- ½ teaspoon baking powder
- Pinch of salt

**Instructions:**

*Make the Filling:*
1. Toss apples with lemon juice in a large bowl
2. Combine ¼ cup flour, sugar, 2 tsp cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg
3. Add to apples and toss well. Set aside

*Make the Crumb Topping:*
1. Use a pastry blender to combine butter, 1 cup flour, brown sugar, 1 tsp cinnamon, baking powder, and salt
2. Work with your hands until it holds together in one clump, then break into crumbs

*Assemble and Bake:*
1. Preheat oven to 375°F
2. Roll out pie crust and place in 9-inch deep dish pie plate. Flute edges
3. Lightly prick bottom of crust with a fork
4. Pour apple filling into crust and press down lightly
5. Sprinkle crumb topping evenly over apples
6. Place pie on a large baking sheet
7. Bake for 25 minutes uncovered
8. Loosely tent with foil and bake 30-35 minutes more until apples are tender and filling is bubbling
9. Cool for 2-3 hours before serving
