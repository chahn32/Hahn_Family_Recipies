# Vanishing Oatmeal Raisin Cookies

*This recipe for Quaker Oats oatmeal raisin cookies is a yummy way to eat your oats. Whether you make them into cookies or bar cookies, vanishing oatmeal raisin cookies will please a crowd or satisfy your sweet tooth.*

## Ingredients

- ½ cup (1 stick) plus 6 tablespoons butter, softened (¾ cup + 2 tablespoons total)
- ¾ cup firmly packed brown sugar
- ½ cup granulated sugar
- 2 eggs
- 1 teaspoon vanilla
- 1½ cups all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon baking soda
- 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- ½ teaspoon salt (optional)
- 3 cups Quaker Oats (quick or old fashioned, uncooked)
- 1 cup raisins

## Instructions

1. Heat oven to 350°F

2. In large bowl, beat butter and sugars on medium speed of electric mixer until creamy

3. Add eggs and vanilla; beat well

4. Add combined flour, baking soda, cinnamon and salt; mix well

5. Add oats and raisins; mix well

6. Drop dough by rounded tablespoonfuls onto ungreased cookie sheets

7. Bake 8 to 10 minutes or until light golden brown

8. Cool 1 minute on cookie sheets; remove to wire rack

9. Cool completely

10. Store tightly covered

## Notes

- Can use either quick oats or old fashioned oats (uncooked)
- Salt is optional
- Called "vanishing" because they disappear so quickly!
- Can be made into bar cookies instead of drop cookies
- Makes 4 dozen cookies

## Personal Notes

### Modifications

- Add ½ teaspoon nutmeg for extra spice
- For chewier cookies, slightly underbake

### Tips
- This is the classic Quaker Oats recipe
- Don't overbake - remove when edges are light golden brown
- Cookies will firm up as they cool
- Perfect with a glass of milk
- Store in airtight container to maintain freshness
