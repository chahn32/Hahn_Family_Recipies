# Beef Bourguignon

*Prep: 15 min | Cook: 3 hrs 30 min | Overnight: 1 day (optional) | Serves: 6*

**For the Stew:**
- ½ pound thick-cut bacon, cut into 1" pieces
- 3 pounds beef chuck, trimmed and cut into 2" cubes
- 1 large white onion, chopped
- 2 large carrots, cut into 2" chunks
- 6 cloves garlic, minced
- ½ cup flour
- 8 sprigs thyme, tied together
- 3 cups Burgundy wine
- 2 large bay leaves
- 3 cups low-sodium beef stock
- ¼ cup tomato paste
- Salt and pepper

**For Finishing:**
- 4 tablespoons butter, divided
- 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
- 16 pearl onions
- 1 pound cremini mushrooms, quartered
- 3 tablespoons flat leaf parsley, minced
- 2 teaspoons fresh thyme leaves
- Salt and pepper

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 350°F with rack in middle
2. Cook bacon in Dutch oven over medium until fat rendered, 7-10 minutes. Remove to paper towel
3. Pat beef very dry, season with salt and pepper
4. Toss with flour, shake off excess (save flour). Sear until browned on all sides. Set aside
5. Leave ¼ cup fat in pot. Add onions, cook 3-5 minutes until softening
6. Add carrots, cook 5-7 minutes until colored
7. Add garlic, cook 1-2 minutes until fragrant
8. Add tomato paste, cook 3-4 minutes, stirring frequently
9. Add remaining flour to pot, stir to incorporate. Cook until no white flour visible, about 2 minutes
10. Slowly add wine and beef stock while whisking to avoid lumps
11. Scrape bottom to remove brown bits
12. Add beef, bacon, thyme, and bay leaves. Turn off heat
13. Cover and bake 2½-3 hours until beef very tender (check at 90 minutes; add ½ cup water if too thick)
14. Remove from oven, taste and season. Discard bay leaves and thyme
15. Skim fat from top
16. If sauce too thin, drain into another pot. Cook over medium-high until thick enough to coat spoon. Recombine. Keep covered
17. Heat large pan above medium. Add olive oil and pearl onions. Cook 4-5 minutes until tender. Remove
18. Add mushrooms, cook 5-8 minutes until browned
19. Add butter, cook until glistening, 2-3 minutes
20. Return onions, toss, season with salt, pepper, and thyme
21. Mix parsley into stew pot
22. Divide mushrooms and onions among plates or mix into pot
23. Serve with mashed potatoes or crusty bread

**Notes:**
- Best if refrigerated overnight and reheated
- Makes 6-8 servings
- Leftovers keep 3 days or freeze 6 months
