# Coq au Vin

*Prep: 15 min | Cook: 2 hrs | Marinate: 2 hrs | Total: 4 hrs 15 min | Serves: 6*

**For the Stew:**
- 4 ounces thick-cut bacon, cut into 1" pieces
- 3½-4 pounds chicken thighs and legs
- 1 medium white onion, diced
- 1 large carrot, diced
- 2 ribs celery, diced
- 5 cloves garlic, minced
- ½ cup flour
- 3 tablespoons tomato paste
- 1 bottle Burgundy wine
- 1 cup chicken stock
- 8 sprigs thyme, tied together
- 2 large bay leaves
- Salt and pepper
- ½ ounce dark chocolate (optional)

**For Finishing:**
- 3 tablespoons butter
- 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
- 16 pearl onions
- 1 pound cremini mushrooms, quartered
- 3 tablespoons flat leaf parsley, minced
- 2 teaspoons fresh thyme leaves
- Salt and pepper

**Instructions:**
1. Trim chicken of excess fat. Place in bowl and pour entire bottle of wine over. Marinate at least 2 hours or overnight
2. Preheat oven to 350°F with rack in middle
3. Cook bacon in Dutch oven over medium until fat rendered, 7-10 minutes. Remove to paper towel
4. Remove chicken from wine (save wine). Pat very dry and season with salt and pepper
5. Dredge in flour, shake off excess. Sear in bacon fat until browned on all sides. Set aside
6. Leave ¼ cup fat in pot. Add onion, carrot, celery. Cook 10-12 minutes until soft
7. Add garlic and cook 2 minutes
8. Add tomato paste and cook 3-4 minutes, stirring frequently
9. Add reserved wine and chicken stock. Bring to boil for 2 minutes, scraping bottom
10. Turn off heat. Add chicken, bacon, thyme, and bay leaves
11. Cover and bake 75-90 minutes until chicken very tender
12. Remove chicken to plate, cover with foil. Discard bay leaves and thyme
13. Skim fat from sauce. Place pot on burner at medium-high
14. Reduce sauce by half, about 10 minutes, until thick
15. Taste and season. Add chocolate and mix until smooth
16. Return chicken to pot, coat with sauce, cover and set aside
17. Heat large pan over medium-high. Add olive oil and pearl onions. Cook 3-5 minutes until tender. Remove
18. Add mushrooms to pan, cook 5-8 minutes until browned
19. Add butter and cook until glistening, 2-3 minutes
20. Return onions to pan, toss, season with salt, pepper, and thyme
21. Mix parsley into pot. Divide mushrooms and onions among plates or mix into pot
22. Serve with mashed potatoes or crusty bread

**Notes:**
- Best if refrigerated overnight and reheated
- Makes 6-8 servings
- Leftovers keep 3 days in fridge or 6 months frozen
